# BUILDING YOUR STAR LIBRARY
## A Guide to Capturing Hard & Soft Skills Through Achievement Stories

---

## 📚 What is a STAR Library?

A **STAR Library** is a structured collection of your professional achievements, each captured using the **SITUATION · TASK · ACTION · RESULT** framework. 

Instead of a CV that lists responsibilities, a STAR Library captures *what you actually did* and *what happened as a result*. During interviews, you'll be asked: *"Tell me about a time when..."* — your STAR Library gives you the exact story, complete with metrics and decision-making.

**Why STAR?**
- Hiring managers test competency through behavioural questions
- Your answers are credible because they're grounded in real projects
- Hard skills (technical, domain-specific) emerge naturally from the actions
- Soft skills (leadership, judgment, integrity) show through your decisions
- You'll deliver the same stories in interviews that you promised in your CV

---

## 🎯 Hard Skills vs Soft Skills in STAR

### Hard Skills
**Definition:** Technical, domain-specific, measurable capabilities.

**Examples:** 
- Leading digital transformation | Implementing cloud infrastructure | Designing data pipelines | Building business cases | Managing vendor contracts | Cyber security compliance | Program delivery | Architecture design

**Where they appear in STAR:**
- **ACTION:** "I implemented AWS-based infrastructure using Terraform and CI/CD pipelines"
- **RESULT:** "System uptime improved from 94% to 99.8%"

---

### Soft Skills
**Definition:** Interpersonal, judgment-based, behavioural capabilities.

**Examples:**
- Strategic thinking | Stakeholder engagement | Change leadership | Integrity under pressure | Systems thinking | Decision-making | Mentoring | Cross-functional collaboration | Communicating complex ideas

**Where they appear in STAR:**
- **SITUATION:** "The board didn't trust IT's capability to deliver"
- **TASK:** "I needed to rebuild confidence while maintaining delivery commitments"
- **ACTION:** "I presented three realistic options with trade-offs, rather than promising everything"
- **RESULT:** "Board approved the phased approach; delivery was on time; trust was restored"

---

## 🏗️ STAR Structure: How to Capture Both

### SITUATION: Set the Scene
**What was the problem or context?**

Your SITUATION should answer:
- What was broken, missing, or constrained?
- Who was affected?
- What was at stake?
- What made this hard? (This is where soft skills emerge: complexity, stakeholder friction, competing priorities)

**Examples:**

❌ *"The IT team was not functioning well."*

✅ *"The IT team of four had not delivered a major project in 18 months. Vendor dependencies had grown to 60% of operational effort. The board was considering outsourcing the function. I had six months to rebuild internal capability and prove the team could deliver independently."*

---

### TASK: Define the Mandate
**What were you specifically asked to do?**

Your TASK should answer:
- What was your role in solving this?
- Were there constraints (budget, timeline, stakeholder friction)?
- What would success look like?
- What trade-offs were you aware of upfront?

**Examples:**

❌ *"I was asked to improve the situation."*

✅ *"As the new CIO, I had to redesign how the team worked — establishing new decision-making norms, rebuilding vendor relationships, and delivering a modernisation program (Azure migration, new project management tool, security framework refresh) in parallel with BAU support for 200+ critical systems. I chose to prioritise team morale and capability over aggressive timeline targets, betting that sustainable pace would deliver more than heroics."*

---

### ACTION: Show Your Thinking & Doing
**What did you actually do? How did you decide? Who did you engage?**

Your ACTION is where you showcase **both** hard and soft skills. It should answer:
- How did you approach the problem?
- Who did you involve and why?
- What technical decisions did you make and why?
- What was your judgment call? (What didn't you do?)
- What was the team dynamic like under pressure?

**Template:**

*"I [conducted / designed / implemented / led] [activity] with [specific people/stakeholders]. My approach was to [specific thinking/methodology]. Rather than [alternative you rejected], I chose to [your decision] because [your reasoning]. I also [parallel work] to ensure [outcome]. When [pressure moment] emerged, I [specific decision under pressure]."*

**Examples:**

❌ *"I implemented AWS cloud infrastructure."*

✅ *"I conducted a structured cloud readiness assessment with the technology leadership team and the business units. Rather than pursuing a 'lift-and-shift' approach (high cost, low benefit), I chose to map applications by workload type — categorising them as 'move as-is', 'refactor for cloud', or 'rebuild cloud-native'. I worked with engineering teams on a pilot refactor using containerisation and microservices patterns, validating the approach before committing the full portfolio. When cost concerns emerged mid-program, I implemented automated environment teardown at 6 p.m. daily, reducing our cloud bill by 40% while maintaining development productivity. The decision involved a conversation with engineering about discipline — we agreed that non-production environments would be ephemeral."*

---

### RESULT: Quantify & Contextualize
**What happened? Who noticed? What evidence do you have?**

Your RESULT should answer:
- What measurable outcomes emerged?
- What feedback did you receive from stakeholders?
- What changed in the organisation as a result?
- What capability did you build?
- What would have happened without your intervention?

**Template:**

*"[Metric 1] improved from [before] to [after]. [Stakeholder] confirmed [impact]. The organisation now [capability gained]. In subsequent [time period], [lasting outcome]."*

**Examples:**

❌ *"The project was successful."*

✅ *"Report turnaround time fell from 48 hours to 6 hours, enabling police to close cases faster. Forensic specialists reported 70% less time on administrative work, redirecting ~200 hours annually to higher-value analysis. Police leadership confirmed that data quality and timeliness improvements were material to case closure rates. Critically, the team remained intact through the program — prior two-year baselines showed 12-month CTO tenure under high-pressure transformations. This team sustained delivery across four years of concurrent regulatory audits and platform modernisation, attributed to the operating culture and sustainable pace established at the outset."*

## 📋 Hard Skills Inventory: How to Spot Them in Your Work

Go through your career and identify the **technical, measurable skills** you've actually used. For each, capture a STAR story:

### Technology & Infrastructure
- [ ] Cloud migration or management (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- [ ] Data centre design or consolidation
- [ ] Infrastructure-as-code (Terraform, Ansible, CloudFormation)
- [ ] Network architecture or redesign
- [ ] Disaster recovery and business continuity
- [ ] Cybersecurity framework implementation (NIST, ISO27001, Essential Eight)
- [ ] Enterprise architecture or system design
- [ ] Vendor evaluation and procurement
- [ ] Budget management and financial planning

### Software & Delivery
- [ ] Software development lifecycle (SDLC) or DevOps
- [ ] Agile or waterfall program delivery
- [ ] Digital product design or platform modernisation
- [ ] Data pipeline or analytics implementation
- [ ] API design or integration
- [ ] Testing strategy or quality assurance
- [ ] Release management or deployment automation
- [ ] Technology stack modernisation

### Data & Analytics
- [ ] Data governance or master data management
- [ ] Business intelligence or data warehouse design
- [ ] Data quality or data integration
- [ ] Regulatory data compliance (GDPR, CCPA, AML/KYC)

### Risk & Compliance
- [ ] Audit or compliance program design
- [ ] Risk assessment or risk register management
- [ ] Policy or control framework development
- [ ] Standards implementation (ISO, NIST, etc.)
- [ ] Security incident response or forensics

**For each box you tick:** Write a STAR story capturing when you did it, what you achieved, and what the organisation gained.

## 💭 Soft Skills Inventory: How to Spot Them in Your Decisions

Soft skills are **visible in the decisions you made**. Go through your career and identify moments where you *chose* a direction:

### Leadership Under Complexity
- [ ] Navigated competing stakeholder priorities
- [ ] Made a decision with incomplete information
- [ ] Changed direction when evidence suggested the original path was wrong
- [ ] Led through uncertainty or ambiguous authority
- [ ] Held a team together through high-stress periods
- [ ] Rebuilt trust after a failure

### Judgment & Integrity
- [ ] Said "no" to a senior leader based on principle
- [ ] Presented bad news clearly to the board
- [ ] Rejected a shortcut that would have looked good short-term but been damaging long-term
- [ ] Made a trade-off decision and stuck to it despite pushback
- [ ] Admitted a mistake and fixed it
- [ ] Chose a harder path because it was more defensible

### Stakeholder Engagement & Influence
- [ ] Changed someone's mind through evidence, not authority
- [ ] Designed a solution that satisfied competing interests
- [ ] Translated technical concepts for non-technical audiences
- [ ] Built a coalition to support an unpopular decision
- [ ] Managed a difficult vendor or partner relationship
- [ ] Engaged subject matter experts to solve a problem outside your expertise

### Systems Thinking
- [ ] Solved a local problem by understanding its place in a larger system
- [ ] Connected dots between departments or functions that weren't talking
- [ ] Designed a solution that improved multiple areas simultaneously
- [ ] Identified a root cause that others had missed
- [ ] Anticipated second-order consequences of a decision

### Development & Mentoring
- [ ] Built capability in your team or organisation
- [ ] Mentored someone into a senior role
- [ ] Developed a person who was initially struggling
- [ ] Created a culture or practice that outlasted you
- [ ] Handed off a function and it thrived without you

**For each box you tick:** Write a STAR story that shows *why* you made the decision you did, *what you were thinking*, and *how it played out*.

## 🛠️ Exercise: Build Your First STAR Entry

Pick one achievement from your career — something you're proud of. Use this template:

```
## [Achievement Title]

**Role:** [Your title at the time]
**Dates:** [Start–End]
**Employer:** [Company]
**Sector/Focus:** [Industry or business area]

**SITUATION**
[What was the problem? Who was affected? What was at stake?]
[Why was this hard? (complexity, stakeholder friction, competing priorities)]

**TASK**
[What were you specifically asked to do?]
[What constraints did you operate under?]
[What would success look like?]

**ACTION**
[What did you actually do?]
[Who did you involve? How did you decide?]
[What was your reasoning?]
[What were you *not* doing and why?]
[What pressure moments emerged? How did you respond?]

**RESULT**
[Metric 1: from [before] to [after]]
[Metric 2: from [before] to [after]]
[Stakeholder feedback: "..."]
[Capability gained by the organisation]
[Evidence: How do you know this worked?]
```

**Time allocation:**
- SITUATION: 3–4 sentences (set the scene)
- TASK: 2–3 sentences (define your role)
- ACTION: 4–6 sentences (show thinking + doing)
- RESULT: 3–4 sentences (quantify + contextualize)

**Total per STAR entry: 300–400 words**

---

## 📊 How Many STAR Entries?

Aim for:
- **15–20 entries** for a 5-year track record
- **25–30 entries** for a 10-year track record
- **One entry per major achievement, project, or decision**

You won't use all of them in every CV. The CV generator will prioritise by relevance to the job posting. But having a rich library means you're prepared for any interview question that starts with *"Tell me about a time when..."*

---

## 🎯 Organizing Your STAR Library

Structure by **employer and role**, not by skill:

```
# Company A — ICT Leadership
## 1. [Achievement 1]
## 2. [Achievement 2]
## 3. [Achievement 3]

# Company B — Operations
## 4. [Achievement 4]
## 5. [Achievement 5]

# Company C — Earlier Roles
## 6. [Achievement 6]
```

This mirrors your CV structure, making it easy to find stories that align to a specific role you held.

## ✅ Quality Checklist: Does This STAR Entry Work?

Before you save a STAR entry, ask:

- [ ] **Is the SITUATION specific?** (Could someone understand what was at stake without asking for clarification?)
- [ ] **Does the TASK define my accountability?** (Am I clear about what I was responsible for?)
- [ ] **Does the ACTION show my thinking?** (Can someone understand why I made the decisions I did?)
- [ ] **Can I defend every claim in the RESULT?** (Do I have evidence — metrics, feedback, outcomes — for what I'm claiming?)
- [ ] **Would I tell this story the same way in an interview?** (Does it feel authentic?)
- [ ] **Does it showcase a hard skill or a soft skill** (or both)? (Is there something a hiring manager would care about?)
- [ ] **Is it written in my voice?** (Would a peer recognise this as my story?)

If you answer "no" to any of these, rewrite before filing.

---

## 🔄 Maintenance: Keeping Your STAR Library Fresh

Add a new entry:
- **After major project completion** (within 2 weeks, while details are fresh)
- **After a difficult decision you're proud of** (especially if it involved judgment calls)
- **When a stakeholder gives you feedback** (e.g., "That was a smart decision to...")
- **Quarterly** (review your past quarter; capture 1–2 new stories)

Retire an entry:
- If you've moved roles and it's no longer relevant to your target audience
- If you now have a better, more recent story that showcases the same skill
- If the achievement is outdated (e.g., a technology you no longer use)

## 💡 Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Vague results:** "The project was successful."  
✅ **Specific results:** "Delivery slipped 4 weeks but came in under budget. The organisation subsequently approved three follow-on projects, all tracking on time."

❌ **Passive voice:** "The system was implemented."  
✅ **Active voice:** "I led the implementation, working with..."

❌ **Unquantified claims:** "Significantly improved performance."  
✅ **Quantified claims:** "Reduced mean time to recovery from 8 hours to 45 minutes."

❌ **Missing the "why":** "We chose cloud."  
✅ **With reasoning:** "We chose cloud because on-premise scaling would have required capex we didn't have. Cloud let us scale elastically with opex."

❌ **No stakeholder voice:** "The project went well."  
✅ **With evidence:** "The business confirmed it was their best deployment experience. Police leadership noted improved data quality."
