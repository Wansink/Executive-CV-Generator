## Notes on Populating This Template

### For Each SITUATION
- **Be specific about the problem** — vague situations produce vague stories
- **Include the stakeholder impact** — who was affected and why it mattered
- **Name the constraint** — budget, timeline, complexity, stakeholder friction, competing priorities
- **Avoid generic openings** — don't start with "The organisation needed..." — start with the real problem

### For Each TASK
- **State your specific accountability** — what were *you* responsible for?
- **Include success criteria** — how would you know if you succeeded?
- **Name the trade-offs** — what constraints or competing priorities did you operate under?
- **Be clear about your mandate** — were you asked to fix it, or did you identify the problem yourself?

### For Each ACTION
- **Show your thinking, not just your doing** — why did you make the decisions you did?
- **Name the people** — who did you involve? Who did you need to persuade?
- **Explain what you chose NOT to do** — most decisions involve trade-offs; make them visible
- **Include pressure moments** — how did you respond when things got hard?
- **Use specific language** — "I implemented AWS CloudFormation..." beats "I used cloud"

### For Each RESULT
- **Quantify everything** — metrics, timelines, percentages, volumes
- **Include stakeholder voice** — what feedback did you receive? How did they react?
- **Name the capability** — what can the organisation now do that it couldn't before?
- **Link to business impact** — did this affect revenue, risk, efficiency, customer experience, team morale?
- **Be honest about limitations** — what worked, what didn't, what would you do differently?

## Maintenance Notes

- **Add new entries quarterly** — within 2 weeks of project completion
- **Review annually** — retire entries that are outdated; keep the best ~20 stories
- **Keep indexed by role** — matching your CV structure makes stories easy to find
- **Be specific about metrics** — generic outcomes won't differentiate you

---

**Total entries to aim for:** 15–20 for a 5-year track record | 25–30 for a 10+ year track record

Every entry is a potential interview question. Build a library that covers your career, and you'll never be caught off-guard.
