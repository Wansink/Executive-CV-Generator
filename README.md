## 📖 How This System Works

### Phase 1: Build Your Source Materials (You do this once)

**A. Master CV**
- Complete career record (all roles, education, credentials)
- Includes budget, team size, geographic scope
- 3,000+ words — this is comprehensive
- Source: `MASTER_CV_TEMPLATE.md`

**B. STAR Library**
- 15–25 achievement entries using SITUATION · TASK · ACTION · RESULT
- Each entry is 300–400 words
- Indexed by role/employer for easy navigation
- Source: `STAR_LIBRARY_TEMPLATE.md`

**C. Position Descriptions**
- One per job you're applying for
- Usually provided by the employer
- The CV generator uses this to prioritise relevant achievements

### Phase 2: Generate Role-Specific CVs (You do this per application)

For each job you apply for:

1. **Feed the system:**
   - Your Master CV
   - Your STAR Library
   - The job's Position Description
   - Your personal details (name, credentials, location)

2. **The generator produces:**
   - 1,000–1,500 word targeted CV
   - Prioritised by relevance to the role's Key Selection Criteria
   - ATS-optimised (includes keywords from the job posting)
   - Interview-ready (stories match STAR framework)

3. **You review & customise:**
   - Read aloud to ensure it sounds like you
   - Check for gaps or inconsistencies
   - Export to Word/PDF for submission

## 💡 Key Principles

### 1. Evidence-Led
Every claim in your CV is sourced from your STAR Library. If the CV says "led digital transformation", your STAR entry explains the specific projects, the business context, your decisions, and the measurable outcomes.

### 2. Role-Specific
Don't submit the same CV to every job. The CV generator prioritises achievements by relevance to each role's KSCs. A role focused on "cyber security" will highlight your CISM background and compliance achievements. A role focused on "digital transformation" will highlight your cloud and modernisation work.

### 3. Credible Decision-Making
Hiring managers test decision-making through questions like:
- "Tell me about a time when you had to make a difficult decision..."
- "How would you approach this problem?"
- "What would you do differently if you had to do it again?"

Your STAR entries answer these by showing your thinking, not just your doing.

### 4. Interview-Ready
Because your CV is drawn from your STAR Library, and your STAR Library is structured around the framework hiring managers use (SITUATION · TASK · ACTION · RESULT), you'll be prepared when they ask "Tell me about a time when..."

## 🛠️ Customisation & Extension

### For a New Role Type
If you're applying for a different role (e.g., CIO → Board Member), update:
1. **Configuration** section with new role title, org name
2. **CORE CAPABILITIES** section with relevant capabilities for the new role
3. The CV generator will re-prioritise your STAR entries based on the new PD

### For a New Sector
If you're moving sectors (e.g., Financial Services → Healthcare), ensure your STAR Library includes:
1. Sector-specific achievements
2. Relevant regulatory or compliance experience
3. Domain knowledge specific to the new sector

### Adding New STAR Entries
After completing a major project:
1. Add a new entry to your STAR Library
2. Next CV generation will have more material to work with
3. Update Master CV annually with new roles and achievements

## ⚠️ Important Notes

### Personal Information & PII
- **Do not include** age, marital status, photo, race, religion, personal references
- **Do include** name, location, phone, email, LinkedIn (for contact purposes)
- **Use location** (city, state) not home address

### Confidentiality
- Anonymise specific client names if required by your employment agreement
- Refer to "major financial services firm" instead of "Westpac" if needed
- Quantify impact without revealing confidential metrics (e.g., "improved efficiency by 25%" not "reduced headcount by 47 people")

### ATS Optimisation
- Use keywords from the job posting verbatim (e.g., if they say "Essential Eight", use "Essential Eight" not "security maturity")
- Keep formatting clean (no graphics, unusual fonts, or creative layouts)
- Use standard section headers that ATS systems recognise

### Verification
- Every metric in your CV should be traceable to a source in your STAR Library
- Be prepared to discuss any achievement in detail
- Avoid overstating or misrepresenting impact

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: How long does it take to build a STAR Library?
**A:** 8–12 hours total. Allocate 20–30 minutes per STAR entry × 15–20 entries. Best done over a few weeks, not all at once.

### Q: Can I use the same CV for multiple roles?
**A:** Not recommended. Each role has different KSCs. The system is designed to generate a new CV per application (takes 2 hours). The investment pays off in relevance and ATS ranking.

### Q: What if I don't have STAR entries for every requirement in the job posting?
**A:** Honest answer: the CV generator will flag gaps. You have two options:
1. Focus on the requirements you *can* demonstrate with evidence
2. Be selective in which roles you apply for

Don't invent achievements. If you don't have evidence, it will show in an interview.

### Q: Should I include metrics that don't make me look good?
**A:** Yes, if they're honest. Example:
- ❌ "Delivered the project on time and under budget."
- ✅ "Project slipped 4 weeks due to scope changes; we came in under budget by re-baselining timelines with the business."

Honesty builds credibility.

### Q: How do I explain a gap or a failed project?
**A:** In your STAR Library, be explicit about lessons learned:
- "The initial approach didn't work because..."
- "In hindsight, I should have..."
- "What I learned for next time was..."

Hiring managers respect self-awareness. Avoid defensiveness.

### Q: Can I use this system for internal promotions?
**A:** Absolutely. In fact, internal applications often have clearer KSCs. Use the same system to tailor your CV to the new role's requirements.
