# QUICKSTART — Executive CV Generator
## Template Version 1.0 | Generic Executive Leadership Track
### Create Your Personalised CV in 4 Steps

---

## 📋 What This System Does

This is a templated, evidence-led CV generation system designed for senior executives. It creates targeted CVs from:
- A **Position Description** (the job you're applying for)
- A **STAR Library** (your achievements and projects)
- A **Master CV** (your full career history)

Output: A professionally formatted, ATS-friendly CV tailored to each role in under 2 hours.

---

## 🚀 Step 1: Gather Your Source Material

You'll need **three documents**:

### (A) YOUR MASTER CV
A comprehensive document with:
- Full name, location, phone, email, LinkedIn
- Professional credentials and certifications
- Complete career chronology (every role, employer, dates)
- Budget and team sizes you've managed
- Technical stack and platforms you've used
- Board and advisory positions (if any)

**Format:** Word document (.docx) or plain text

---

### (B) YOUR STAR LIBRARY
A structured achievement library using the **SITUATION · TASK · ACTION · RESULT** framework.

**What goes in:**
- One STAR entry per major project or achievement
- Quantified results: metrics, percentages, volumes, timelines
- Problem state (before) → Outcome state (after)
- Decisions made and trade-offs considered
- Stakeholder levels engaged (board, CEO, executive, team)
- Behavioural signals: accountability, integrity, systems thinking, leadership

**Why STAR?** Hiring managers test you on these during interviews. Your CV should contain the same stories you'll tell. When asked "Tell me about a time you...", you're already prepared.

**Format:** Word document (.docx) with one section per role/employer

---

### (C) POSITION DESCRIPTION
The job posting or role specification for the role you're applying for.

**Include:**
- Job title and organisation name
- Key Selection Criteria (KSC) or required capabilities
- Technical skills, frameworks, certifications mentioned
- Reporting line and team scope
- Any keywords that appear multiple times (ATS signals)

**Format:** .docx, .pdf, or pasted text in `<pd_text>...</pd_text>` tags

## 📝 Step 3: Run the CV Generator

Provide the system with:
1. Your **Master CV** document
2. Your **STAR Library** document
3. The **Position Description**
4. Your **personal details** (from Step 2)

The generator will:
- **Step 0:** Extract all evidence from your three source documents
- **Step 1:** Map Key Selection Criteria from the PD to achievements in your STAR library
- **Step 2:** Prioritise achievements by relevance to the role
- **Step 3:** Draft the CV in 1,000–1,500 words
- **Step 4:** Audit for evidence — verify every claim has a source
- **Step 5:** Self-verify — check for gaps, inconsistencies, and ATS signals

## 💡 Tips for Success

### Master CV Structure
```
[Name & Credentials]
[Location | Phone | Email | LinkedIn]

[Executive Profile — 2–3 sentences]

[Core Capabilities Table]

[Professional Experience]
  - [Company] | [Role] | [Dates]
    - [Achievement 1 — with metrics]
    - [Achievement 2 — with metrics]
    - [Achievement 3 — with metrics]

[Earlier Roles — summary format]

[Certifications & Qualifications]

[Board / Advisory Roles]

[Technical Stack]
```

### STAR Library Structure
```
[Employer Name] — [Sector / Focus Area]

## [Project/Achievement Title]
**Role:** [Your role]
**Dates:** [Start–End]

**SITUATION**
[The problem or context — what was broken or missing?]

**TASK**
[What were you asked to do?]

**ACTION**
[What did you actually do? Who did you engage? What was your thinking?]

**RESULT**
[What happened? Metrics? Outcomes? Feedback from stakeholders?]
```

### Position Description Keywords
As you read the PD:
- Highlight any skill, framework, tool, or certification mentioned **more than once**
- Flag phrases that repeat (e.g., "strategic thinking", "stakeholder engagement", "digital transformation")
- These are ATS signals — use them verbatim in your CV if they match your experience

### Spanning Multiple Roles
If your STAR achievement spans multiple employers (e.g., you changed companies mid-project):
- Assign it to the role where you spent the most time on the achievement
- In the STAR entry, note: "Project began at [Company A], continued and completed at [Company B]"

---

## ⚠️ What NOT to Include

❌ Filler language ("responsible for", "key member of", "worked on")  
❌ Unquantified claims ("led transformation", "improved performance")  
❌ Gaps in reasoning (why did you make that decision?)  
❌ Generic corporate buzzwords without evidence  
❌ Personal information (age, photo, marital status, race, religion)  

✅ Instead: Specific achievements with numbers, clear decision-making, and stakeholder impact.

## 📞 Support

**Common questions:**

**Q: What if I have gaps in my Master CV?**  
A: Fill them now. The generator works from what you provide. Include budget, team, and scope for every role.

**Q: What if my STAR library is too long (50+ entries)?**  
A: Good problem. The generator will prioritise by relevance to the PD. But in practice, 15–25 entries covering your last 5–10 years is ideal.

**Q: Can I use the same CV for multiple roles?**  
A: Not recommended. Each role has different KSCs. Run the generator for each application — it takes 2 hours per CV.

**Q: How many words should the CV be?**  
A: 1,000–1,500 words for the body (excluding header/footer). This allows ATS systems to scan all content and hiring managers to read in 3–5 minutes.
