## Checklists, Workflows & Troubleshooting

## 📋 FIRST-TIME USER CHECKLIST

### Week 1: Setup & Understanding
- [ ] Read `README.md` (30 minutes) — Understand the system
- [ ] Read `QUICKSTART_PROMPT.md` (20 minutes) — Learn the workflow
- [ ] Skim `EXAMPLES/` folder (15 minutes) — See what output looks like
- [ ] Review `STAR_LIBRARY_INSTRUCTIONS.md` (30 minutes) — Understand STAR framework
- [ ] Review `MASTER_CV_TEMPLATE.md` structure (15 minutes) — Know what you're building

### Week 2–3: Build Your Master CV
- [ ] Copy `MASTER_CV_TEMPLATE.md` into a Word document
- [ ] Fill in your header (name, credentials, location, contact)
- [ ] Write your executive profile (2–3 sentences with quantified scope)
- [ ] List your core capabilities (10–15 across leadership and technical)
- [ ] Fill in professional experience (last 15 years, role by role)
- [ ] Add education, certifications, board roles, technical stack
- [ ] Save as `Your_Name_Master_CV.docx`
- [ ] Spend 5–8 hours on this (don't rush)

### Week 3–4: Build Your STAR Library
- [ ] Copy `STAR_LIBRARY_TEMPLATE.md` into a Word document
- [ ] Read `STAR_LIBRARY_INSTRUCTIONS.md` again (now it will make sense)
- [ ] Identify 15–20 major achievements from your career
- [ ] For each achievement, write a STAR entry:
  - [ ] SITUATION (what was the problem? 2–3 sentences)
  - [ ] TASK (what were you asked to do? 2–3 sentences)
  - [ ] ACTION (what did you do? How did you decide? 4–5 sentences)
  - [ ] RESULT (what happened? Metrics? 3–4 sentences)
- [ ] Use the quality checklist in instructions after each entry
- [ ] Save as `Your_Name_STAR_Library.docx`
- [ ] Spend 8–12 hours on this (one entry every 45 minutes)

### Ready to Apply
- [ ] You now have your Master CV ✅
- [ ] You have your STAR Library ✅
- [ ] You're ready to generate CVs for each job posting

## 🎯 APPLICATION WORKFLOW (Per Job Posting)

### 0. Read the Job Posting (15 minutes)
- [ ] Read the entire job posting carefully
- [ ] Highlight all Key Selection Criteria (KSCs)
- [ ] Highlight technical skills, frameworks, certifications named
- [ ] Note any keywords that appear multiple times
- [ ] Understand seniority level and reporting structure

### 1. Prepare Your Source Material (15 minutes)
- [ ] Have your Master CV ready (Word document)
- [ ] Have your STAR Library ready (Word document)
- [ ] Copy/paste the job posting into a text file for reference
- [ ] Note the role title and organisation name

### 2. Run the Generator (60–90 minutes)
- [ ] Open `CV_GENERATOR_PROMPT.md`
- [ ] Fill in the CONFIGURATION section with your details
- [ ] Attach or provide your Master CV
- [ ] Attach or provide your STAR Library
- [ ] Paste the job posting in `<pd_text>...</pd_text>` tags
- [ ] Type "Generate my CV" and wait for the system to complete
- [ ] The system will follow 5 steps:
  - Step 0: Extract evidence from your documents
  - Step 1: Prioritise achievements by relevance to the role
  - Step 2: Draft 1,000–1,500 word CV
  - Step 3: Audit claims against source documents
  - Step 4: Self-verify and prepare output

### 3. Review & Customise (30 minutes)
- [ ] Read the generated CV aloud (does it sound like you?)
- [ ] Check that every claim is traceable to a STAR entry
- [ ] Ensure all Key Selection Criteria are addressed
- [ ] Check for spelling and grammar
- [ ] Customise any language that doesn't sound natural
- [ ] Export to PDF (if required by application) or keep as Word

### 4. Submit (5 minutes)
- [ ] Upload to job application system
- [ ] Ensure format matches requirements (PDF, Word, plain text)
- [ ] Double-check file name has your name and role

**Total time: 2–2.5 hours per CV**

## 🛠️ TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: "My CV feels too generic"
**Cause:** You're not using specific language or quantified results.  
**Fix:** Go back to your STAR entries. Replace:
- ❌ "Led a transformation" with ✅ "Led a 18-month transformation that reduced reporting time from 48 hours to 6 hours"
- ❌ "Managed stakeholders" with ✅ "Negotiated agreements across three sovereign agencies with competing priorities"

### Problem: "The generated CV doesn't address a Key Selection Criterion"
**Cause:** You don't have evidence (STAR entry) for that requirement.  
**Fix:** Two options:
1. Be selective about which roles you apply for (apply for roles you can demonstrate)
2. Add new STAR entries if you have undocumented achievements

**Don't invent achievements.** If you lack evidence, it will show in interviews.

### Problem: "I'm getting too many findings in the audit step"
**Cause:** Your Master CV or STAR Library has inconsistencies (dates, figures, names).  
**Fix:** 
- Go back to the source documents and verify accuracy
- Update Master CV and STAR Library to match reality
- Re-run the generator

### Problem: "My CV is longer than 1,500 words"
**Cause:** Too many achievements or too much detail per achievement.  
**Fix:**
- Trim earlier roles to summary format (1–2 sentences)
- Keep recent roles (last 5 years) detailed
- For each achievement, remove supporting details that aren't essential

### Problem: "The system says a claim can't be verified"
**Cause:** You mentioned something in the CV that doesn't appear in your STAR Library or Master CV.  
**Fix:**
- Add the missing achievement to your STAR Library
- Or remove it from the CV
- Never make claims you can't back up with evidence

## ✅ QUALITY CHECKLIST (Before You Submit)

### Content Quality
- [ ] Every metric is accurate (no rounding up, no invented figures)
- [ ] Every achievement is evidenced (traceable to a STAR entry)
- [ ] Decision-making is visible (I understand *why* you made choices)
- [ ] Stakeholder impact is clear (I know who was affected and how)
- [ ] Tone is appropriate for seniority level (executive, not junior)

### Structure Quality
- [ ] CV is 1,000–1,500 words (not too short, not too long)
- [ ] Recent roles are detailed (2–3 achievements each)
- [ ] Earlier roles are summarised (1–2 sentences each)
- [ ] All Key Selection Criteria are addressed
- [ ] Keywords from job posting are included naturally (not forced)

### ATS Quality
- [ ] Layout is clean (no graphics, unusual fonts, or creative formatting)
- [ ] Plain text version is readable (copy-paste test)
- [ ] All important keywords are present
- [ ] Formatting would survive an ATS scan

### Interview Readiness
- [ ] For each achievement mentioned, I could tell a 2-minute STAR story
- [ ] My story would match what I wrote in the CV
- [ ] I could defend every metric with examples
- [ ] I'm comfortable discussing trade-offs and decisions

## 🎯 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### In Master CV
❌ Vague job descriptions ("responsible for", "key member of")  
✅ Specific accomplishments with scope ("Led transformation of X systems for Y users")

❌ Missing budget or team size  
✅ "Managed budget of $X million | Team of X staff"

❌ Undated achievements  
✅ All achievements tied to specific roles and timelines

### In STAR Library
❌ Generic SITUATION ("The team wasn't functioning")  
✅ Specific context ("The team had not delivered a major project in 18 months, creating board concerns about outsourcing the function")

❌ Passive ACTION ("It was implemented")  
✅ Active, decision-rich ACTION ("I chose to build internally rather than buy, because...")

❌ Unquantified RESULT ("The project was successful")  
✅ Specific RESULT ("Delivery slipped 4 weeks due to scope changes; we came in under budget through re-baselining; stakeholder satisfaction improved from 5.2/10 to 7.8/10")

### In Generated CV
❌ Filler language that sounds corporate  
✅ Authentic language that sounds like you

❌ Trying to shoehorn every STAR entry into one CV  
✅ Selecting 3–5 most relevant entries for this specific role

❌ Copying STAR text verbatim into the CV  
✅ Adapting STAR stories into narrative form (shorter, more polished)

## 💡 TIPS FOR SUCCESS

### Building Your Master CV
- Spend time getting the profile right (2–3 sentences is all you get to make first impression)
- Include numbers for everything (budget, team size, scope, timeline)
- List every role for the past 15 years (even if you only detail recent ones)
- Be honest about what you achieved and what happened

### Building Your STAR Library
- Write STAR entries within 2 weeks of project completion (while fresh)
- Start with 3–5 entries, then build up to 15–20 over time
- Don't edit too much initially (get the story down, refine later)
- Ask a trusted peer to review your STAR entries (do they make sense?)

### Generating a CV
- Read the job posting multiple times (you'll notice different things each time)
- Mark up the job posting with KSCs and keywords
- Match STAR entries to KSCs before running the generator
- Review the output for authenticity (does it sound like you?)

### Before Submitting
- Read your CV aloud (catch awkward phrasing, check flow)
- Test plain text version (copy-paste to check ATS compatibility)
- Export to PDF (check formatting hasn't broken)
- Get peer review if time permits (20 minutes of feedback is worth it)

## 📞 WHEN YOU'RE STUCK

**Q: I don't know if my STAR entry is good enough**  
A: Use the quality checklist in `STAR_LIBRARY_INSTRUCTIONS.md`. Answer every question honestly. If you answer "no" to any, rewrite.

**Q: I'm not sure if my Master CV is detailed enough**  
A: Look at `Sample_Master_CV_Alex_Johnson.md`. Your entries should be similar in depth.

**Q: The generator found inconsistencies in my documents**  
A: Go back to source and verify accuracy. Update the documents. Re-run.

**Q: I'm applying for a role I'm not 100% qualified for**  
A: Focus on the capabilities you *do* have. Apply for roles where you can demonstrate 70%+ of requirements with evidence.

**Q: My STAR Library is too long (30+ entries)**  
A: Good! But in any single CV, you'll only use 3–5. The full library means you're prepared for any interview question.

**Q: I'm changing careers/sectors**  
A: Ensure your STAR Library includes achievements relevant to the new sector. If you lack them, you may not be ready for the transition yet (or need to accept lower seniority/salary to make the move).
