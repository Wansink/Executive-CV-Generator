### Chief Information Officer | TechGov Solutions Pty Ltd
*2018 – Present | Brisbane, QLD*

**Team:** 8 direct reports; 35-person ICT team  
**Budget:** $4.2m BAU | $2.1m project portfolio  
**Clients:** Government agencies, regulated private sector organisations

#### Key Achievements & Responsibilities

**ISO27001 Information Security Management System Implementation & Accreditation**
- **Context:** Organisation had fragmented security controls across three legacy business units acquired over five years. No unified Information Security Management System. Clients (government agencies) increasingly demanding SOC 2 Type II and ISO27001 compliance evidence as a contract requirement.
- **Action:** Designed and implemented a unified ISMS across all three business units over 18 months. Conducted security risk assessments across all systems. Established governance forums with business unit leaders and external security consultants. Developed security policies, incident response procedures, and control frameworks mapped to ISO27001 Annex A. Managed the external audit process and remediation of findings.
- **Impact:** Achieved ISO27001 accreditation (first time, zero major findings). SOC 2 Type II audit passed. Retention rate for government contracts improved by 35% as clients confirmed compliance posture. Security incident response time reduced from 8 hours to 2 hours. Staff security training completion rate reached 98%.

**Cross-Agency Data Sharing & Interoperability Platform**
- **Context:** Three government agency clients each managed separate systems for case management and information exchange. Data duplication, manual reconciliation, and delays in cross-agency investigation coordination. No formal data governance framework for shared information assets.
- **Action:** Established a cross-agency working group with CIOs, business owners, legal, and privacy officers from all three agencies. Designed a federated data governance model that respected each agency's data ownership while enabling secure, audit-logged information exchange. Built an API-based interoperability layer connecting legacy systems without requiring system replacement. Negotiated data sharing agreements and incident response protocols. Conducted privacy impact assessments and obtained formal approvals from privacy commissioners.
- **Impact:** Investigation coordination time reduced by 40%. Duplicate data entry eliminated across agencies. Cross-agency data accuracy improved from 87% to 98%. Three agencies now using the platform as their standard operating procedure. Recognised by state government as a model for other whole-of-government initiatives.

**Vendor Risk & Contract Governance Framework**
- **Context:** Portfolio of 47 vendors providing software, cloud infrastructure, security, and managed services. No central contract register, inconsistent Service Level Agreements, no formal vendor risk assessments. Compliance obligations varied by client contract, creating confusion and risk of non-compliance.
- **Action:** Developed a vendor risk assessment matrix (security posture, financial stability, compliance certifications). Renegotiated 28 critical vendor contracts to include standardised SLAs, incident notification requirements, and audit rights aligned to ISO27001 and client requirements. Established a quarterly vendor review cadence with executive sponsorship. Built a vendor risk dashboard accessible to board and executive.
- **Impact:** Vendor-related security incidents reduced from 6 per year to 0 (over 3-year period). Contract compliance cost reduced by 18% through renegotiation of renewal terms. Board gained visibility of vendor risk for the first time. No compliance failures attributed to vendor issues in subsequent audits.
